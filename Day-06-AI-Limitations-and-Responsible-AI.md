# Week 1 - Day 6: AI Limitations and Responsible AI

## What are AI Limitations?

AI tools are useful, but they are not always correct. They can make mistakes because they generate answers based on patterns in data.

## 1. Incorrect Information

AI may give an answer that sounds correct but is actually wrong.

### Example
AI may provide an incorrect current CEO name or outdated company information.

### Solution
Verify important information using reliable sources.

---

## 2. Hallucinations

AI can create false or made-up information, such as fake links, research papers, or facts.

### Solution
Ask for sources and check them before using the information.

---

## 3. Bias

AI may give unfair or unbalanced answers because its training data may contain bias.

### Example
An AI system should not judge a person based on gender, religion, caste, or background.

---

## 4. Privacy

Do not share sensitive personal information with AI tools.

### Avoid sharing
- Passwords
- OTPs
- Bank details
- Aadhaar or PAN details
- Private company documents

---

## What is Responsible AI?

Responsible AI means using AI in a safe, fair, transparent, and ethical way.

## Principles of Responsible AI

- Use AI fairly.
- Protect user privacy.
- Verify important answers.
- Avoid harmful or biased content.
- Keep humans responsible for final decisions.

## Real-Life Example

If AI helps a company shortlist resumes, a human recruiter should review the final decision. AI should support people, not make unfair decisions alone.

## Key Takeaways

- AI can make mistakes.
- AI hallucinations can create false information.
- Important facts should be verified.
- Never share sensitive information with AI.
- Responsible AI means using AI safely and fairly.
